## 3. Agile Manifesto Values and Key Scrum Principles

Agile is a modern approach to software development that focuses on flexibility, collaboration, and delivering value through continuous improvement. It was introduced through the Agile Manifesto, which outlines four key values that guide how development teams build and deliver software efficiently.

The first Agile value is **Individuals and interactions over processes and tools**. This emphasizes that successful software development depends more on the people involved than on strict processes or complex tools. Effective communication, collaboration, and teamwork help developers solve problems faster and produce better results. When team members interact frequently and share ideas openly, projects move forward more smoothly and efficiently.

The second value is **Working software over comprehensive documentation**. Traditional development methods often focus heavily on creating detailed documentation before building the product. Agile, however, prioritizes delivering functional software that users can interact with and evaluate. While documentation still has importance, Agile considers working software as the main measure of progress. Delivering usable features early helps teams gather feedback and improve the product continuously.

The third value is **Customer collaboration over contract negotiation**. Agile encourages ongoing interaction with customers, stakeholders, or users throughout the development process. Instead of defining all requirements at the beginning and strictly following them, Agile teams frequently communicate with users to understand their needs and gather feedback. This collaboration ensures the final product solves real problems and meets user expectations.

The fourth value is **Responding to change over following a plan**. In many projects, requirements evolve due to market changes, user feedback, or new insights. Agile recognizes that change is unavoidable and encourages teams to adapt quickly. Rather than rigidly sticking to an initial plan, Agile teams remain flexible and adjust their work as new information becomes available.

To implement Agile effectively, many teams use the **Scrum framework**, which organizes work into short development cycles called **sprints**. A sprint typically lasts between one and four weeks and focuses on delivering a small but valuable portion of working software. This approach allows teams to show progress regularly and receive feedback early in the development process.

One key Scrum concept is the **Product Backlog**, which is a prioritized list of all features, improvements, and tasks required for the project. From this list, the team selects specific items to work on during a sprint. These selected tasks form the **Sprint Backlog**, which represents the work planned for that sprint cycle.

Another important Scrum principle is **incremental delivery**. At the end of each sprint, the team should produce a working version of the product that can be reviewed, tested, or demonstrated to stakeholders. This ensures that development progress is visible and allows feedback to be incorporated early, reducing the risk of major issues later.

Scrum also promotes **continuous improvement** through regular reviews and retrospectives. During these meetings, the team evaluates what worked well and identifies areas for improvement. By reflecting on their processes and collaboration, teams can refine their workflow and become more efficient over time.

Overall, Agile values and Scrum principles help development teams remain flexible, focused on delivering value, and committed to continuous improvement. By working in short cycles, collaborating closely with stakeholders, and adapting to change, teams can build software that better meets user needs while maintaining productivity and quality.
